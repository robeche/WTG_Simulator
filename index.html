1. Fundamental Assumptions

Actuator disc theory models the wind turbine rotor as an infinitesimally thin, permeable disc that extracts energy from the flow. Key assumptions:

  • Incompressible flow: Air density ρ remains constant (valid for wind speeds ≪ speed of sound)
  • Steady state: Time-averaged quantities; neglects transient effects
  • Axisymmetric: Flow is uniform across the disc and radially symmetric
  • No rotation: Simplified 1D momentum theory (ignores wake rotation)
  • Streamtube: Air that passes through the rotor forms an isolated streamtube

Actuator disc theory

The rotor is modeled as a permeable actuator disc that slows the air down. The axial induction a measures how much the wind speed is reduced at the rotor plane relative to the free stream. The stream tube contracts upstream and expands in the wake because the air slows down (mass conservation). The Betz limit is the value of a that maximizes the extracted power.

What BEM Does

Blade Element Momentum combines airfoil aerodynamics and actuator-disc momentum. For each pair (λ, β), it computes a steady operating point and returns rotor-level loads and performance.

Per-annulus equations

$$\tan(\phi) = \frac{V_\infty (1-a)}{\Omega r (1+a')}\,,\qquad \alpha = \phi - (\beta + \theta(r))$$
$$dT = \tfrac12 \rho W^2 c\,(C_l\cos\phi + C_d\sin\phi)\,B\,dr$$ $$dQ = \tfrac12 \rho W^2 c\,(C_l\sin\phi - C_d\cos\phi)\,B\,r\,dr$$

Rotor totals and coefficients

$$T = \int_{r_{hub}}^{R} dT\,,\qquad Q = \int_{r_{hub}}^{R} dQ\,,\qquad P = \Omega Q$$
$$C_P = \frac{P}{\tfrac12 \rho A V_\infty^3}\,,\qquad C_Q = \frac{Q}{\tfrac12 \rho A R V_\infty^2}\,,\qquad C_T = \frac{T}{\tfrac12 \rho A V_\infty^2}$$

How matrices are built

Sweep a grid of tip-speed ratio and pitch values. At each grid point solve BEM and store:

$$C_P[i,j] = C_P(\lambda_i,\beta_j),\quad C_Q[i,j] = C_Q(\lambda_i,\beta_j),\quad C_T[i,j] = C_T(\lambda_i,\beta_j)$$

These lookup matrices are then used by controller and simulation modules for fast interpolation during time-domain runs.
